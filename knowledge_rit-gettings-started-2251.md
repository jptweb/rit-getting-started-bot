# Academic Related Activities

* Academic related activities are defined as below

* This definition serves purposes relating to financial aid (See [Student LOA’s/Withdrawals](#h.6agdrbwykzbh))

Participating in a class where there is an opportunity for direct interaction between the instructor and students; submitting an academic assignment; taking an exam, completing an interactive tutorial, or participating in computer-assisted instruction; participating in a study group that is assigned by the school; participating in an online discussion about academic matters; and initiating contact with a faculty member to ask a question about the academic subject studied in the course.

Academically related activities DO NOT include activities where a student may be present but not academically engaged, such as: living in institutional housing, participating in the school’s meal plan, logging into an online class without active participation, participating in academic counseling or advisement.

(Source:  Department of Education Federal Student Aid Handbook, Withdrawals and Return to Title IV Funds)

### Students asking faculty directly:

* If a student asks you to add them to a class, direct the student to contact IGM Advising:

### What about co-ops? (from email 2023-11-10)

* Students should not be enrolled in classes after add/drop if they intend to take a full-time co-op in that term

# Contracts (Faculty)

With the October break quickly approaching, the Provost’s Office has received questions about RIT’s expectations for faculty when classes are not in session. As a result, I would like to review the university’s faculty meeting guidelines.

[Policy E 04.0](https://www.rit.edu/academicaffairs/policiesmanual/e040), Section III.B states:

Faculty on a 9-month appointment “…are expected to be available consistent with their departmental assignments and program responsibilities for the entire period of time when university classes are officially in session and during the entire final exam period of each term in addition to the week prior to the start of the fall term and week following the end of the spring term.”

The bolded portion of this policy notes that faculty are not obligated to participate in administrative activities such as department meetings when the university is open but classes are not in session. This includes Reading Day, a day when faculty are asked to be available to support students preparing for final exams. Therefore, I ask that you refrain from scheduling faculty meetings or other activities on days when classes are not in session at RIT.

* Less common situations:

* Sabbatical: see <https://www.rit.edu/policies/e010>. The faculty member likely has 100% scholarship and 0% teaching, or possibly a mix of service and scholarship. As such, the evaluation would consider those forms of course release.
* Retirement Transition: faculty still has a PoW and still must submit an evaluation.
* Medical Leave: employees are eligible for full merit and by policy, they do not work. There could be circumstances for less than full merit, but those situations must be discussed with HR and the Dean. The evaluation does not include personal information about the leave because of confidentiality.  Suggested text: “the review only covered times for which the faculty member was expected to perform the duties listed (teaching, scholarship, service).”
* Family Leave: see Medical Leave. There are circumstances in which the faculty member may have work, which becomes work that the unit head evaluates.
* Permanently Leaving RIT (retirement, termination, end of contract, etc): put something in the file that explains when the faculty member left.

* If you do not submit your evaluation by the assigned due date, unit heads will write the evaluation based on available information.

* If a faculty member refuses to meet, the unit head will still provide an evaluation based on available information.
* RIT expects unit heads to document interactions (or lack thereof) in the absence of input from faculty.

* Track committees, task forces, and effort (e.g., hours, “cadence” of meetings). Perhaps you had a leadership role and a significant outcome.

# Law Enforcement (external)

RIT regularly receives requests for student and employee information from external law enforcement officials in the normal course of business, e.g., for security clearances required for federal employment, or site visits to confirm international student visa information. Occasionally, outside law enforcement agencies may also call or come to campus in person seeking access to records, facilities, employees, or students, when conducting investigations or enforcement actions.

RIT approaches any interactions with law enforcement with these key principles in mind:

* The safety, security, and well-being of our students, faculty, and staff will always be our top priority.
* RIT is committed to ensuring that law enforcement actions on campus adhere to due process, including requiring a warrant or subpoena when necessary.

Following these guidelines will help maintain the safety and security of our campus environment, and ensure RIT legally complies while protecting the rights and privacy of all individuals involved.

If you, as a faculty or staff member, receive communication or an in-person visit from any external law enforcement officials or agency requesting access to records, facilities, another employee, or a student, including U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) officers, you should:

Remain Calm and Professional

* Stay composed to avoid escalating any interactions. Do not run or flee.
* Always avoid taking any action to physically obstruct any law enforcement officers, including ICE agents.

Immediately Contact[Public Safety](https://www.rit.edu/publicsafety/)

* Public Safety can be reached by calling 585-475-3333, or by texting them at 585-205-8333. Public Safety will work with the Office of Legal Affairs to determine whether the agents possess a validly issued judicial warrant or subpoena, as necessary.

Know Your Rights and Responsibilities

* Do not consent to searches or provide access to RIT non-public areas. Contact Public Safety and they will ensure that proper authorization is provided, if necessary. Law enforcement or ICE agents can freely enter RIT public spaces such as lobbies, dining areas, or parking lots.
* You have the right to remain silent during any encounter with law enforcement, including ICE, if the agents ask you for personal information.
* If you believe that you are the subject of an ICE investigation, you have the right to ask for legal counsel. The Office of Legal Affairs provides information on attorney referrals for personal matters. [Learn more.](https://www.rit.edu/fa/legalaffairs#legal-referrals)

Protect Privacy and Confidentiality

* Do not share information about the visit with unauthorized individuals.
* Ensure that any information provided to law enforcement agents, including ICE agents, is done in compliance with university policies and legal requirements.
* Remember that RIT must comply with the law, including by protecting student information as required by the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA).[Learn more.](https://www.rit.edu/policies/c070)

* Secure all office doors and windows. Close window blinds and secure portable office equipment whenever possible. Take personal valuables home, and set the security alarm system prior to leaving campus for the holidays. Pre-arranging a ‘last one out locks the office door’ plan will bolster after-hours security.   Also, please remember to remove/secure any loose hanging objects (Holiday decorations, paper affixed to walls, etc) as they sometimes can cause false alarms in areas where there are motion detectors.
* Now is a good time to inspect door locks and latches in your office/suite, and contact Facilities Management Services immediately at 475-6771 if repairs need to be made.
* Laptop computers and other portable electronic devices; petty cash, master keys, vital files, and procurement cards should be secured behind two locked doors. * Turning off and unplugging holiday lights, coffee pots (check the water line to your coffee makers), crock pots and other appliances used during office celebrations will reduce the risk of fire.
* Avoid having merchandise delivered to the office during the break so that it’s not left unattended and unsecured.

# Research Assistants (Stipend, typically GRAs)

General info: [On-campus Supervisors | Career Services and Co-op | RIT](https://www.rit.edu/careerservices/employers/on-campus-supervisors)

This communication is intended to provide an overview of the role of a graduate research assistant (GRA) and to clarify the associated hours of effort and expectations during university breaks and time off.

### Classification of Graduate Research Assistants

Graduate research assistants (GRAs) are considered “students” and not employees of the University. Per the U.S. Department of Labor, no employment relationship exists between a GRA and the University. GRAs must meet the following requirements: 1) matriculated in a Masters or PhD degree program; 2) conducting original, professional-level research which is generally associated with fulfilling the requirements of external grants; 3) the research is performed under the supervision of an RIT faculty member; and 4) the research environment is provided by RIT.

### Maximum of 20 hours per week of effort allowed

GRAs can give a maximum of 20 hours of effort per week towards a faculty member’s research as part of their GRA appointment. This time limit does not include time spent by a GRA on their own research, to satisfy the requirements for an advanced degree.

### University breaks and time off

GRAs are on break when the RIT academic calendar indicates a break, including during observed university holidays and during break weeks between academic terms. Academic terms covered by an appointment are considered active times for research, but University departments may provide GRAs more time off at the discretion of the faculty advisor. However, stipend payments cannot be reduced or modified as a result of taking additional time off. SPA should be contacted in situations where there are requirements from external funding source(s) for GRA appointments and the GRA has a need for an extended leave that may have a negative impact on the completion of the project’s research outcomes.

### Stipends are not paychecks

GRAs receive a semi-monthly stipend payment through the accounts payable process based on the level of effort committed at the time the appointment is made. Stipend payments are not an hourly wage or salary. Deductions may not be made based on a GRAs performance or hours performed towards a faculty member’s research. If a concern arises regarding satisfactory progress on research, faculty must address the concern with the student to facilitate successful progress.

GRA payments and expectations have academic and legal implications. This communication is also being shared with graduate students; GRAs are asked to notify the Graduate School if they are asked to provide more than 20 hours of effort per week towards a faculty member’s research or if students are asked to be present during the aforementioned break periods.

If you have any questions regarding this communication, feel free to reach out to me at dcsrgs@rit.edu.

### Vehicles:

Give the plows distance. Do not drive too close to a plow or try to pass. The plow’s weight and size make it difficult to maneuver or stop quickly and create blind spots. The safest place is behind the plow, where the roads are clear.

* Help maximize the plow’s efforts by parking near other vehicles to leave as many open areas as possible for the plow to access. Once plows have cleared a section of the lot, move your car to a cleared spot close to other vehicles so the plow may clear the covered spots. No standing or driving in fire lanes.
* Try to drive on cleared roads only. Driving on snow-covered roads will compact the snow, making it slippery and dangerous for other drivers.
* Make sure to fully clear off your car – including the roof, all windows, headlights and taillights. Snow on vehicles can create blind spots for you and sudden hazardous white-out conditions for others when it blows off.
* As always – Do Not Drive Distracted! Winter weather can worsen driving conditions, limiting distractions and making focusing on driving more critical.

### Additional Tips to Stay Safe:

* Make sure to dress warmly and in layers.
* Be careful getting in and out of vehicles.
* Just like with driving – walk on cleared paths! Walking on snow-covered paths will compact the snow and create slippery conditions.
* Assume all wet or dark spots will be slippery.

# Student Funding For Clubs

Information from Sandy Johnson:

As we continue to support our student clubs and organizations, I wanted to remind you how to respond to funding requests from these groups.

It is essential that we maintain a unified and collaborative approach to ensure student clubs and organizations meet established expectations. These include developing a detailed budget, actively fundraising, engaging with the Student Government or Greek Finance Committee, and consulting with their Organizational Advisor and/or Campus Life or Club Sports Advisor before seeking additional support from campus leadership, departments and alumni.

These procedures are not just about securing funds but are an essential part of the learning experience for students. Through this process, they gain valuable skills in financial planning, resource allocation, and navigating challenges. In some cases, they may need to adjust their expectations or scale back their plans based on available resources.

If a student club or organization approaches you for funding, please ask them to provide details on their existing funding sources and a complete budget for the program. Additionally, we encourage you to contact the Center for Campus Life (Courtney Bringley, cebccl@rit.edu) to verify the organization's and the event's status. This helps ensure the club is in good standing and has initiated the appropriate event approval processes. Campus Life (or Club Sports) can also confirm budget needs based on what has already been allocated or is available to the group should the students not provide this information or if you want to validate the details.

By working together, we can achieve our collective goal of helping students involved in clubs and organizations grow in their understanding of responsible stewardship and organizational management while ensuring equitable support for all groups across our campus community

Technical Operations will be taking over the printing to the wide format printer. We will be doing this for several reasons.

# Winter

RIT Sends the following:

### Walk Cautiously

* Use cleared paths when walking around campus
* Walk with short, flat strides keep your center of gravity between your feet
* Assume all wet/dark areas are slippery or ice
* Wipe shoes/boots before entering a building to keep the entrance dry
* Report slippery conditions to FMS by calling the RIT Service Center at 585-475-5000
* Report accidents to[RIT Public Safety](https://www.rit.edu/publicsafety/#contact)

### Driving and Parking

Please be aware that snow removal efforts may temporarily block parking lots and driving lanes. To ensure areas are cleared efficiently, we ask that you park in designated parking spaces and avoid areas not typically used for parking such as fire lanes and loading docks. These spaces are essential for our grounds team to move and pile snow. In addition:

* Take your time in snowy/icy conditions
* Keep the proper distance between you and the vehicle in front of you (3 seconds)
* Always wear your seatbelt
* Go slowly when getting in and out of your vehicle – this is when most slips happen
* Use the correct tires and keep them properly inflated
* Check belts, hoses, battery and fluids regularly
* Keep the fuel tank at least half-full

### Weather-related Closings

Severe weather conditions, such as significant snowfall, ice, wind, and extreme cold, can lead to unsafe conditions both on and off-campus. In the event of a weather-related closing or delay, RIT will utilize various communication channels to inform the community. These include:

* The RIT homepage
* Email and text notifications from RIT Alert
* X (@RITtigers)
* Facebook
* Coordination with local television and radio stations

Please make sure to monitor these channels regularly for updates. For more information about RIT Alert and to add or update your cell phone number, visit[RIT Alert Emergency Notification System](http://www.rit.edu/fa/buscont/content/rit-alert-emergency-notification-system).
